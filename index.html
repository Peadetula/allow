The Insight

The gate is not a security problem.
It is an information problem.

Guards are already doing their job correctly. They're trained to stop what they can't verify — and they should. The issue was never their judgment. It was that no one ever gave them the information to use it. Allow doesn't replace security. It gives security something worth trusting.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What is Allow?
Allow is a verification layer for apartment gates. It gives guards, residents, and delivery platforms the information they need to confirm a delivery is real, in seconds, without a call or a walk downstairs.
How does Allow work?
When a rider arrives, the guard scans a code linked to that order. Allow confirms the rider's identity, the destination, and that the order is genuine — and notifies the resident automatically.
Does Allow replace security guards?
No. Guards make the final decision at every gate. Allow simply gives them accurate information to make that decision with confidence, instead of guesswork.
Who is Allow built for?
Residents, building and property managers, security companies, delivery platforms, and riders — everyone who touches a delivery between the road and the front door.
When will Allow launch?
We're preparing our first pilot across a small group of Nairobi apartment buildings. Waitlist members will be the first to know as dates are confirmed.
