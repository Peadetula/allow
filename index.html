The problem

The last mile
has a wall.

Everything about ordering online is fast and modern, right up until it meets a residential gate. Then it falls apart in the same four steps, every time.

  1. 01

    The rider arrives with a confirmed order and nothing to prove it is real.

  2. 02

    The guard has no information — no name, no unit, no way to verify.

  3. 03

    The resident walks down, or the rider is turned away, or the order times out.

  4. 04

    Everyone loses — a refund, a bad rating, a rider who skips the next order.

The insight

The gate is not a security problem.
It is an information problem.

Guards are not the obstacle. They are trained to stop what they cannot verify, and they should. The problem was never their judgment. It was that nobody ever handed them the information to use it. Allow does not replace security. It gives security something worth trusting.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What is Allow?

Allow is a verification layer for apartment gates. It gives the guard, the resident, and the delivery platform the information they need to confirm a delivery is real, in seconds, without a phone call or a walk downstairs.

How does it actually work?

When a rider arrives, the guard scans a code tied to that order. Allow confirms the rider's identity, the destination unit, and that the order is genuine, then notifies the resident automatically.

Does Allow replace the security guard?

No. The guard makes the final decision at every gate. Allow simply replaces guesswork with accurate information so that decision is made with confidence.

Who is it built for?

Residents, building and property managers, security companies, delivery platforms, and riders. Everyone who touches a delivery between the road and the front door.
